The successor to RPGSim, Music Quest is a role-play game in which players can battle random monsters, earn experience to level up, get coins to buy new items, and proceed through the world until eventually beating the final boss Rapula. This game was coded in Java and uses a graphics library made by Dr. Gregory Neat, my AP Computer Science teacher in 9th grade. There's a lot of stolen images and music in the game, but I'm not making any money off of it and I am not that good at composing music. My brother likened it to Runescape, in which opening the game with the intent to play it will get boring quickly, but having it on the side while doing something else allows the player to progress and pay more attention to key moment like new areas and bosses.

Based Beats is a rhythm game I made using PyGame, a library made for games with graphicsi in Python. The game is quite similar to other rhythm games: "maps" with chart and music files are loaded when the user selects them, the player uses 4 keys (QWOP is the default) to hit notes falling on 4 different columns, and they gain a different amount of points based on their accuracy. Where my game shines is overall customizations. Users can adjust delay for audio, change keys, adjust note colors, and create their own charts. Everything but the last one can be done in-game, and even creating charts has a simple format.

At the top of the text file is the BPM. Each note is 1/8th of a beat, so songs with 1/16th notes need to double the BPM to keep every note.
Each line of text after should have 4 numbers:
1 is a normal note.
2, 3, and 4 are the start, middle, and end of held notes respectively.
5 is a negative note, which punishes the player for hitting it.
For example:
1000
0100
0010
0001
starts with one note at the very left, and then a note on a column to its right until it reaches the rightmost column.

You can download the game on Github.
You can find a demonstration video on Youtube.
