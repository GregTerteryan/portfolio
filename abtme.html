About Me

Hi! My name is Greg Terteryan! I'm an Armenian American, and if you've seen other parts of my portfolio, you know that I'm quite the computer... enthusiast?

My love for computer technology goes way back to when I was a child. It wasn't anything like "I got to see how computers were made and what make them tick," it was more like, "I saw a computer, pondered for a little bit, and then wondered how they worked."

While my curiousity for how they worked wasn't a big priority then, I was still fascinated and in love with the fact that they worked. Such an incredible culmination of knowledge from many different people, all coming together to create such a wonderful tool! That and my undying love of video games is what set the stage for where I am now.

I'm also quite interested in the ocean and marine life. Just the fact that organisms have been able to adapt in many different ways to such a vast array of odd environments. I'm also a big proponent of using nature as an inspiration for engineering, and the many organic mechanisms used by marine life could also be used by humans to make things more efficient.

Some fun facts about me:

My favorite color is red, my two favorite fonts are Comfortaa and Power Clear (which is probably obvious), and my favorite video game is Super Smash Bros. Ultimate. I also love Minecraft and Roblox for the incredible amount of things that other super creative and smart people have made on it. One of my friends helped develop a Roblox game called Combat Initiation, and it's my favorite one on the platform. I also have a YouTube channel where I make Roblox animations, edits of games I play, and memes.

For those who haven't seen me, I am actually quite fat, which is something I've working to change for over 2 years now. I've been regularly going to the gym, only skipping it when school REALLY starts to pick up. I actually enjoy working out, and weightlifting is one of my favorite activities. However, I really hate it when a bunch of people are using all of the machines I plan to use that day. When that happens, I just go do another half hour of cardio to round it out to a full hour. Should I have my own personal gym, I would probably spend a LOT more time in it, and maybe even have a dedicated Workout Sunday every week.
